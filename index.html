AVERAGEIFS: Conditional Average (Multiple Criteria)

The **AVERAGEIFS** function calculates the average (arithmetic mean) of cells that meet **multiple** specified criteria. It is the advanced, versatile version of AVERAGEIF.

**Key Difference from AVERAGEIF:** The **average_range** argument comes *first* in AVERAGEIFS.

---

Formula Syntax

=AVERAGEIFS(average_range, criteria_range1, criteria1, [criteria_range2, criteria2], ...)
---

Arguments (Parts of the Formula)

  • average_range: The actual cells to average (e.g., the column containing grades or prices). This is the **required first argument**.
  • criteria_range1: The first range to apply a test against (e.g., Column A, listing Departments).
  • criteria1: The first condition (e.g., `"Marketing"`).
  • [criteria_range2, criteria2], ...: *Optional*. Subsequent pairs of ranges and their corresponding criteria.
---

Example üí°

Find the average commission amount (Column D) for the "West" region (Column A) **AND** for employees who have been with the company for over 5 years (Column C):

=AVERAGEIFS(D:D, A:A, "West", C:C, ">5")
