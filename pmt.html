PMT: Calculating Periodic Loan Payments

The **PMT** function calculates the **periodic payment** for a loan based on constant payments and a constant interest rate. This is the function you use to figure out your monthly mortgage or car loan payment.

**Crucial Rule:** The result of the PMT function is generally displayed as a **negative number** because it represents a cash outflow (money you pay).

---

Formula Syntax

=PMT(rate, nper, pv, [fv], [type])
---

Arguments (Parts of the Formula)

  • rate: The **interest rate per period**. Always divide the annual rate by the number of payment periods per year (e.g., annual rate / 12 for monthly payments).
  • nper: The **total number of payment periods** in the loan (e.g., loan term in years * 12 for monthly payments).
  • pv: The **Present Value**, or the total amount the series of future payments is worth now (i.e., the loan principal or the amount borrowed).
  • [fv]: *Optional*. The **Future Value**, or the cash balance you want after the last payment is made. Default is 0 (the loan is fully paid off).
  • [type]: *Optional*. When payments are due: **0** for the end of the period (default), **1** for the beginning of the period.
---

Example üí°

Calculate the **monthly payment** for a **$20,000** car loan over **5 years** with a **6% annual interest rate** (where payments are monthly):

=PMT(6%/12, 5*12, 20000) → Result: **-$386.66** (The monthly payment)
