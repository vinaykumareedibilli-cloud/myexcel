DATEDIF: Calculating the Difference Between Dates

The **DATEDIF** (Date Difference) function is a hidden legacy function in Excel that calculates the **number of whole days, months, or years** between a start date and an end date.

**Key Use:** Calculating accurate age, employee tenure, or the exact duration of time-based metrics.

---

Formula Syntax

=DATEDIF(start_date, end_date, unit)

**Important:** The `start_date` must always be chronologically **before** the `end_date`.

---

Arguments (Parts of the Formula)

  • start_date: The initial date (e.g., date of birth, start of employment).
  • end_date: The final date (e.g., today's date, resignation date).
  • unit: A code, entered as text in quotation marks, specifying the type of interval to return:
Unit Code Returns Example Use
**"Y"** The number of **complete years** in the period. Calculating a person's exact age.
**"M"** The number of **complete months** in the period. Total months of service.
**"D"** The number of **days** in the period. Total days elapsed.
**"YD"** The number of **days remaining** after the last full year. Days until next birthday.
**"YM"** The number of **months remaining** after the last full year. Months of service past the last full year.
**"MD"** The number of **days remaining** after the last full month. Days left in the current month segment.
---
