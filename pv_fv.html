PV & FV: Time Value of Money

**PV (Present Value)** and **FV (Future Value)** are core financial functions used to calculate the time value of money, which states that money available today is worth more than the same amount in the future.

**Crucial Rule:** In Excel finance functions, cash paid out (e.g., deposits, payments) must be entered as a **negative number**. Cash received (e.g., loan proceeds, final value) is positive.

---

1. PV Function (Present Value)

Calculates the **current value** of a series of future payments or a single future lump sum, discounted at a specific rate.

=PV(rate, nper, [pmt], [fv], [type])
  • rate: The interest rate per period (e.g., annual rate / 12 for monthly payments).
  • nper: The total number of payment periods (e.g., years * 12 for monthly payments).
  • [pmt]: *Optional*. The payment made each period (e.g., loan repayment).
  • [fv]: *Optional*. The future value balance you want to attain after the last payment.

Example üí° (PV)

How much can you borrow today (the present value) if you pay $500 monthly for 5 years at 4% annual interest?

=PV(4%/12, 5*12, -500)
---

2. FV Function (Future Value)

Calculates the **future value** of an investment based on periodic, constant payments and a constant interest rate.

=FV(rate, nper, [pmt], [pv], [type])
  • rate: The interest rate per period.
  • nper: The total number of payment periods.
  • [pmt]: *Optional*. The payment made each period (e.g., monthly savings deposit).
  • [pv]: *Optional*. The present value (a lump sum investment made today).

Example üí° (FV)

What will your investment be worth after 10 years if you start with a $1,000 lump sum and deposit $100 monthly at 6% annual interest?

=FV(6%/12, 10*12, -100, -1000)
