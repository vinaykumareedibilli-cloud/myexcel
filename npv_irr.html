NPV & IRR: Investment Decision Tools

**Net Present Value (NPV)** and **Internal Rate of Return (IRR)** are two methods used to analyze the profitability of an investment or project by considering the time value of money.

**Cash Flow Rule:** Cash flows (payments/receipts) must be ordered chronologically, and the initial investment (cash outflow) should be a **negative number**.

---

1. NPV Function (Net Present Value)

Calculates the sum of the present values of a series of future cash flows, discounted at a user-specified rate.

=NPV(rate, value1, [value2], ...)
  • rate: The discount rate over the length of one period (e.g., your required rate of return or cost of capital).
  • value1, [value2], ...: The cash flows that occur at the end of each period. These must be cash flows starting from period 1 (Year 1, Month 1, etc.).

Example üí° (NPV - The Correct Way)

If the initial investment (Year 0) is -$10,000 (in cell A1), and cash flows for Years 1-5 are in cells B1:F1. **Note:** The initial investment must be added separately to the NPV result.

=A1 + NPV(10%, B1:F1)

**Decision Rule:** Accept the project if the NPV is **greater than 0**.

---

2. IRR Function (Internal Rate of Return)

Calculates the **discount rate** at which the Net Present Value (NPV) of all the cash flows from a particular project equals zero.

=IRR(values, [guess])
  • values: A range of cells containing the cash flows. This range **must include the initial investment (Year 0)** as the first value.
  • [guess]: *Optional*. Your initial guess for the rate of return. Default is 0.1 (10%).

Example üí° (IRR)

Calculate the IRR for a project where the initial investment (-$10,000) and all future cash flows (Years 1-5) are in cells A1:F1:

=IRR(A1:F1)

**Decision Rule:** Accept the project if the calculated IRR is **greater than** your required rate of return (or cost of capital).
