RATE: Calculating the Interest Rate Per Period

The **RATE** function calculates the **interest rate per period** for an annuity. Since the rate returned is per period (e.g., monthly), you must **multiply the result** by the number of periods per year (ee.g., *12) to get the Annual Percentage Rate (**APR**).

**Key Use:** Finding the effective interest rate on a loan or determining the yield on an investment.

---

Formula Syntax

=RATE(nper, pmt, pv, [fv], [type], [guess])
---

Arguments (Parts of the Formula)

  • nper: The **total number of payment periods** in the loan or investment.
  • pmt: The **payment** made each period. This must be a **negative number** (cash outflow).
  • pv: The **Present Value** (PV), or the total amount borrowed/invested today. For a loan, this is the principal (positive number).
  • [fv]: *Optional*. The **Future Value**. Default is 0.
  • [guess]: *Optional*. Your initial guess for the rate. Default is 10%.
---

Example üí° (Calculating APR on a Loan)

A $10,000 loan is paid off with monthly payments of $200 over 5 years. Calculate the annual interest rate:

=RATE(5*12, -200, 10000) * 12 → Result: **~9.70%** (The annual rate)

**Explanation:** The raw `RATE` result is the monthly rate. We multiply by 12 to annualize it. The payment (`-200`) is negative (outflow), and the principal (`10000`) is positive (inflow/money received).
